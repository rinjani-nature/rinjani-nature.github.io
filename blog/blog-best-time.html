Best Time to Visit Mount Rinjani

By Rinjani Nature Team | 5 Min Read

Choosing the right time to trek Mount Rinjani is the most important decision you will make for your trip. The weather on a 3,726m volcano is unpredictable, but knowing the seasonal patterns can mean the difference between a glorious sunrise view and a wet, cloudy struggle.

In short: The trekking season runs from April to December. But when exactly is the "perfect" time? Let's break it down.

1. Official Opening Dates

First, you must know when the park is legally open. Mount Rinjani National Park is usually CLOSED from January 1st to March 31st every year.

  • Reason: Heavy monsoon rains make the trails dangerous (landslides), and this period allows the ecosystem to recover ("nature healing").
  • Implication: You cannot book a trek during these months.

2. The Dry Season (May – October)

This is widely considered the best time to visit.

Pros:

  • High Visibility: Clear blue skies and the best chance to see the Segara Anak Lake and neighboring islands (Bali/Sumbawa).
  • Safety: Trails are dry and not slippery.
  • Star Gazing: Incredible views of the Milky Way at night.

Cons:

  • Dust: The trails (especially Sembalun) get very dusty. Bring a buff/mask.
  • Cold: It is colder at night (down to 0°C) compared to the wet season.
  • Crowds: July and August are "Peak Season". Expect more hikers on the trail.

3. The Shoulder Season (April, November, December)

These are the transition months between the wet and dry seasons.

  • April & May: The landscape is incredibly green and lush after the rainy season. This is the most beautiful time for photography, although there might be occasional afternoon showers.
  • November & December: The rain starts to return. Trekking is still possible, but you must be prepared for rain, especially in the afternoon. The trails can be slippery.

4. Month-by-Month Breakdown

Month Weather Condition Recommendation
Jan - Mar Heavy Rain / Storms Closed
April - May Clear/Green Scenery Highly Recommended (Beautiful Views)
Jun - Aug Dry / Windy / Cold Excellent (Best for Summit)
Sep - Oct Very Dry / Dusty Good (Prepare mask)
Nov - Dec Rain Showers Okay (Bring rain gear)

5. A Special Note on Full Moon

Advanced tip: Try to schedule your trek during a Full Moon. The moonlight illuminates the mountain at night, making the summit attack at 2 AM easier and magical. However, if you prefer stargazing (Milky Way), choose a New Moon date.

Conclusion

The absolute best time for clear skies is June to August. For greener scenery and fewer crowds, aim for April or May. Whenever you choose to go, Rinjani Nature ensures you have the right equipment to handle the weather.
