Senaru vs. Sembalun: Which Route is Best?

By Rinjani Nature Team | Jan 26, 2026

Mount Rinjani is one of Indonesia’s most iconic trekking destinations. Rising dramatically to 3,726 meters above sea level, this active volcano on Lombok Island offers challenging trails, breathtaking landscapes, and an unforgettable sense of adventure.[cite: 2]

When planning a Mount Rinjani trek, one of the most common questions is: Should you choose the Senaru route or the Sembalun route?[cite: 2]

Understanding the Two Main Rinjani Routes

Both routes are officially recognized entrances to Mount Rinjani National Park, and each offers a unique trekking experience. Before comparing them in detail, it’s important to understand the basic difference:[cite: 2]

  • Sembalun Route: Widely known as the best path to reach the summit.[cite: 2]
  • Senaru Route: Famous for its spectacular crater rim views overlooking Segara Anak Lake.[cite: 2]

1. Sembalun Route: The Best Choice for the Summit

The Sembalun route starts from Sembalun Village, located at approximately 1,150 meters above sea level. Because of its higher starting point and gradual incline, this route is considered the safest and most effective option for summit attempts.[cite: 2]

The trail passes through wide open savanna landscapes, rolling hills, and volcanic terrain. While the scenery may seem repetitive to some, it provides excellent visibility and easier pacing.[cite: 2]

Pros of the Sembalun Route

  • Best and most recommended route for summit attempts.[cite: 2]
  • Gradual elevation gain, suitable for first-time Rinjani hikers.[cite: 2]
  • Open landscapes with wide views.[cite: 2]
  • Stunning sunrise at the summit.[cite: 2]

Cons

  • Very exposed to sun and wind (can be very hot).[cite: 2]
  • Less vegetation compared to Senaru.[cite: 2]

2. Senaru Route: The Most Scenic Trek

The Senaru route begins in Senaru Village, at around 600 meters above sea level. This lower starting point means the climb is steeper, especially on the second day, but the reward is incredible scenery.[cite: 2]

The trail leads through dense tropical rainforest, home to monkeys, birds, and lush vegetation. As you climb higher, the forest opens up to reveal breathtaking views of Segara Anak Lake and the dramatic crater walls.[cite: 2]

Pros of the Senaru Route

  • Rich and diverse landscapes (Jungle & Lake view).[cite: 2]
  • Cooler temperatures in forest sections.[cite: 2]
  • Stunning crater rim panoramas.[cite: 2]

Cons

  • Steeper and more physically demanding.[cite: 2]
  • Slippery trails during the rainy season.[cite: 2]
  • Less ideal for direct summit attempts.[cite: 2]

Difficulty Comparison

Difficulty depends largely on your trekking objective:[cite: 2]

  • Senaru Route: Shorter but much steeper, requiring strong leg endurance.[cite: 2]
  • Sembalun Route: Longer but more gradual, though the summit push (summit attack) is extremely demanding due to loose sand.[cite: 2]

Final Verdict: Which one to choose?

There is no single best route for everyone.[cite: 2]

  • Choose Sembalun if your dream is to stand on the Summit of Mount Rinjani.[cite: 2]
  • Choose Senaru if you want the most scenic Crater Rim & Lake views without the extreme summit push.[cite: 2]
